Glossary

Welcome to our Glossary Page! Here, you'll find a comprehensive list of terms and definitions related to Printing and Packaging Industry. Whether you're a newcomer looking to familiarize yourself with key concepts or an experienced professional seeking a quick reference, our glossary is designed to provide clear and concise explanations. Explore the alphabetically organized terms below to enhance your understanding and navigate through the terminology associated with our field. Happy exploring !

A

  • Accordion Fold: A type of fold where each section of a printed piece is folded in alternating directions.

B

  • Bleed: Printing that goes beyond the edge of where the sheet will be trimmed, ensuring there is no white space at the edges.
  • Boxboard: A thick paperboard used for packaging boxes.
  • Brochure: A promotional document containing information about a company, product, or service.
  • Bulk Pack: Packaging items together without individual wrapping.
  • Barcode: A machine-readable code consisting of parallel bars.

C

  • CMYK: Abbreviation for the four process colors: Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Key (black).

D

  • Die-cutting: The process of cutting paper into unique shapes using a die.
  • Dot Gain: The increase in dot size that occurs during the printing process.
  • Duotone: An image reproduced using two colors, often for artistic effect.

E

  • Embossing: Creating a raised design or image on paper using heat and pressure.

F

  • Foil Stamping: The application of a metallic or colored foil to paper.
  • Flexography: A printing method using flexible rubber plates, commonly used for packaging materials.
  • Folding Carton: A box made from paperboard, usually for packaging goods.
  • Fiberboard: A type of paperboard made from recycled paper fibers.

G

  • Ghosting: Unintended faint images that appear on a printed piece.
  • Gang Run Printing: Printing multiple jobs simultaneously to save costs
  • Gloss Varnish: A glossy coating applied to enhance the appearance of printed materials.
  • Gravure Printing: A high-quality printing method using engraved cylinders.
  • GSM: Grams per square meter, a measure of paper weight.

H

  • Hickey: A spot or imperfection in printing caused by dust or debris.
  • Halftone: An image reproduced using dots of varying sizes to create shades of gray.
  • Hot Stamping: A printing process that uses heat and pressure to apply foil or a hologram

I

  • Imposition: The arrangement of pages on a press sheet to ensure proper printing, folding, and binding. Imposition is crucial for producing a correctly ordered final product during the printing process.

J

  • JIT (Just-In-Time): A production strategy that aims to minimize inventory.
  • JPEG: Joint Photographic Experts Group, a common file format for images.

K

  • Kerning: Adjusting the space between characters in typography.
  • Kraft Paper: Strong, brown paper used for packaging.

L

  • Lamination: Applying a thin layer of plastic to paper for protection and appearance.
  • Laser Die Cutting: Using a laser to cut intricate shapes in paper or other materials.
  • Letterpress: A traditional printing method using raised metal type.
  • Ligature: A combination of two or more characters into a single glyph.
  • Line Screen: The number of lines or dots per inch on a halftone screen.
  • Lithography: Printing using a flat surface with ink and water.

M

  • Matte Finish: A non-glossy surface on printed materials.
  • Metallic Ink: Ink containing metallic particles for a shiny effect.

N

  • Non-Impact Printer: A printer that does not physically strike the paper.

O

  • Offset Printing: A printing technique where ink is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket, then to paper.

P

  • Pantone Matching System (PMS): A standardized color matching system.
  • Perfect Binding: A bookbinding method where pages are glued to a cover.
  • Plate: A metal or paper sheet with an image for printing.
  • Proof: A test print to check for errors before final printing.

Q

  • QR Code: A two-dimensional barcode for storing information.

R

  • Raster Image: An image made up of pixels.
  • Registration Marks: Crosshair symbols used to align printing plates.
  • Resolution: The clarity and detail of an image, measured in dots per inch (DPI).
  • Rotogravure: A printing method using a rotary press and engraved cylinders.

S

  • Saddle Stitching: Binding by stapling through the spine.
  • Scoring: Creating a crease in paper to facilitate folding.
  • Substrate: The material to which ink is applied, such as paper or cardboard.

T

  • Thermography: A printing process that creates a raised image using heat.
  • Trapping: Overlapping colors slightly to prevent gaps in printing.

U

  • UV Coating: A high-gloss coating applied and cured with ultraviolet light.

V

  • Vapor Barrier: Material used to prevent moisture transmission.
  • Vignette: A gradual fade from a color or transparency to the background.

W

  • Waterless Printing: A printing method that eliminates the need for water in the press.
  • Watermark: A design impressed into paper during manufacturing.
  • Wire-O Binding: A binding method using double-loop wire.

X

  • X-height: The height of lowercase letters excluding ascenders and descenders.
  • Xerography: A dry photocopying process using toner and heat.

Y

  • Yellowing: The gradual discoloration of paper or other materials over time.

Z

  • Zip Strip: A tear-off strip on packaging for easy opening.
